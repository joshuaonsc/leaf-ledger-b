How evasion works, and how Leaf Ledger helps
In MapleStory Idle PvP, hits work roughly like this: while an attacker's accuracy stays above the target's evasion, hits land. Once evasion catches up to accuracy, the attacker starts to miss, and the miss rate climbs as evasion pulls further ahead.

The catch is that late-game you can't tell from the match page whether someone has stacked evasion. Combat power alone stops being a reliable tell. Leaf Ledger reads it empirically: log who you fight, tick the evasion box when you see MISS markers, and search a name before you challenge it. You get a verdict: AVOID (you've lost to them before, so refresh past), CAUTION (only wins on record, but they run evasion and may have grown stronger since you last beat them, so it's no guarantee), or FAVORABLE (clean wins, no evasion seen).

Everything stays in your browser. Link a .json file (Chrome/Edge) to autosave changes to disk, or use Import / Export to back up and share.
